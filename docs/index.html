Vi-SparkRL: Enhancing Tonal Accuracy and Naturalness in Vietnamese TTS via Multi-Objective Rewards

Abstract. Recent advances in LLM-based TTS have enabled high-quality zero-shot voice cloning, yet tonal languages such as Vietnamese remain challenging due to their lexical tone system. We propose Vi-SparkRL, the first RLHF framework specifically designed to preserve Vietnamese tonal integrity in lightweight (0.5B) zero-shot TTS models. Our approach applies Group Sequence Policy Optimization (GSPO) to the acoustic modeling phase, effectively mitigating training instability seen with token-level GRPO for continuous audio signals. We further design a multi-objective reward mechanism integrating ASR-based intelligibility (WER), speaker similarity (SIM), acoustic quality (UTMOS), and a novel VietTone reward that aligns synthesized F0 trajectories with native tonal patterns. Evaluations on the PhoAudiobook dataset demonstrate that Vi-SparkRL surpasses state-of-the-art TTS systems in intelligibility while maintaining competitive naturalness and speaker similarity.

1. Method

1.1 Group Sequence Policy Optimization (GSPO)

To enhance the prosody and naturalness of the baseline Vi-SparkTTS-0.5B model, we employ GSPO, a variant of GRPO that eliminates the need for a separate value function by utilizing group-relative advantages. For each prompt, a group of G sequences is generated. The advantage for each sequence i is estimated by comparing its reward Ri against the mean reward of the group:

Âi = ( Ri − mean(R) ) / std(R)     (1)

Unlike GRPO, which computes advantages at the token level and suffers from high-variance importance weights in long-sequence generation, GSPO applies sequence-level importance sampling, providing improved stability and scalability for continuous audio outputs.

1.2 Multi-objective Hybrid Reward System

The total reward combines four components scaled to [−1, 1] to facilitate stable gradient updates:

Rtotal = 2.0 × (∑i ∈ {mos, sim, wer, tone} wi · ri) − 1.0     (2)
  • UTMOS — Acoustic Quality (w = 0.4): UTMOS22-Strong predicted MOS, normalized from [1,5] to [0,1]: rmos = (clip(smos, 1, 5) − 1) / 4
  • SIM — Speaker Similarity (w = 0.3): ECAPA-TDNN + WavLM cosine similarity: rsim = (cos(eref, egen) + 1) / 2
  • WER — Intelligibility (w = 0.2): Whisper Large V3 word error rate: rwer = 1.0 − min(WER(textref, texthyp), 1.0)
  • VietTone — Tone Accuracy (w = 0.1): Positional match rate of Unicode tone marks between reference and hypothesis transcription

1.3 VietTone Reward

Standard TTS models struggle with Vietnamese lexical tones, where semantic meaning depends heavily on F0 contours. The VietTone reward computes the positional match rate of tone marks extracted via Unicode normalization:

rtone = (∑j=1L 𝟙[T(textref)j = T(texthyp)j]) / L     (6)

where T(s) extracts tone marks from string s using Unicode normalization (mapping tones to {0,…,5}), and L is the number of tones in the reference.

1.4 Training Setup

We utilize a curated subset of PhoAudiobook (941 hours), filtering utterances with WER > 10%, yielding 14,000 premium samples partitioned into 10,000 (GSPO training), 2,000 (validation), and 2,000 (zero-shot test). SFT is first performed with LoRA (r=16, α=32, dropout=0.05) on the Vi-SparkTTS-0.5B backbone. For GSPO, the converged SFT adapters are merged into base weights as the reference policy πref, and a fresh set of LoRA adapters is trained as the active policy πθ. During training, K=8 candidate trajectories are sampled per prompt (temperature=0.9, β=0.01, ε=3×10−4, lr=1×10−6). All experiments run on 4×NVIDIA A100 (80GB) GPUs via Accelerate.
